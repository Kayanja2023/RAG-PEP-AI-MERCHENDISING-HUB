# Hollard Insurance Claims Process Guide

## Understanding Claims

A claim is a formal request to your insurance company for payment based on the terms of your insurance policy. When an insured event occurs (death, disability, illness, accident, theft, damage), you or your beneficiaries can submit a claim to receive the benefits you're entitled to.

## General Claims Principles

### Act Quickly
- Report claims as soon as possible after the event
- Most policies have time limits for notification
- Delays can complicate the process or affect payout
- Contact your broker immediately

### Be Honest and Accurate
- Provide truthful information
- Don't exaggerate or minimize facts
- Disclose all relevant details
- Fraud can void your entire policy

### Document Everything
- Keep copies of all documents submitted
- Take photos of damage
- Get police reports when applicable
- Save all correspondence
- Track dates and reference numbers

### Know Your Policy
- Understand what's covered
- Be aware of exclusions
- Know your excess/deductible amounts
- Check waiting periods
- Review coverage limits

## Life Insurance Claims

### When to Claim
Submit a death claim when the insured person passes away, whether from natural causes, illness, or accident (subject to policy terms).

### Required Documents

**Standard Documentation:**
1. Completed death claim form
2. Original death certificate (BI-1663 form from Department of Home Affairs)
3. Certified copy of deceased's ID
4. Certified copies of beneficiaries' IDs
5. Proof of banking details for beneficiaries
6. Original policy document or policy number
7. Last premium payment proof

**Additional Documents (Depending on Circumstances):**
- Medical records and hospital reports
- Doctor's statement
- Police report (for unnatural death)
- Post-mortem/autopsy report
- Inquest findings (if applicable)
- Accident report
- Toxicology reports (if applicable)

### Claims Process Timeline

**Day 1-3: Immediate Notification**
- Contact your broker immediately
- Broker registers the claim with Hollard
- Receive claim reference number
- Get list of required documents

**Week 1: Document Submission**
- Gather all required documents
- Submit through your broker
- Broker reviews for completeness
- Documents forwarded to Hollard

**Week 2-4: Assessment**
- Claims assessor reviews documentation
- May request additional information
- Medical review if required
- Investigation if circumstances warrant

**Week 4-6: Decision & Payment**
- Claim approved or declined
- Beneficiaries notified of decision
- Payment processed if approved
- Funds transferred to beneficiaries' accounts

**Note:** Simple claims can be processed in 2-3 weeks. Complex claims involving unusual circumstances, large sums, or investigations may take 2-3 months or longer.

### Types of Life Insurance Claims

**Natural Death Claims:**
- Death from illness or natural causes
- Typically straightforward
- Medical records required
- Fastest processing time

**Accidental Death Claims:**
- Death from unexpected accidents
- Police report required
- Accident circumstances investigated
- Post-mortem usually required

**Death During Waiting Period:**
- Most policies have waiting periods (3-6 months) for natural death
- Accidental death usually covered immediately
- Check your specific policy terms

**Suicide Claims:**
- Most policies exclude suicide in first 12-24 months
- After exclusion period, suicide may be covered
- Police and inquest reports required
- Longer investigation process

## Disability Claims

### Temporary Disability Claims

**When to Claim:**
When you're unable to work due to illness or injury for the duration specified in your policy (often after 1-3 month waiting period).

**Required Documents:**
- Disability claim form
- Medical certificate from treating doctor
- Specialist reports (if applicable)
- Employer confirmation of absence
- Proof of income
- Bank details

**Ongoing Requirements:**
- Regular medical updates
- Confirmation of continued disability
- Proof of treatment
- May require independent medical examinations

**Payment:**
- Monthly income replacement (typically 75% of salary)
- Paid monthly during disability period
- Continues until recovery or retirement age
- Regular reviews required

### Permanent Disability Claims

**When to Claim:**
When you're permanently unable to work in your occupation due to illness or injury.

**Required Documents:**
- Permanent disability claim form
- Comprehensive medical reports
- Specialist assessments
- Occupational therapist reports
- Employer statements
- Income verification

**Assessment:**
- More extensive than temporary disability
- Independent medical examination likely required
- Occupational capacity assessment
- May take several months to process

**Payment:**
- Lump sum or monthly payments (depending on policy)
- Calculation based on policy terms
- Tax treatment varies

## Critical Illness Claims

### When to Claim
When you're diagnosed with one of the critical illnesses specified in your policy (cancer, heart attack, stroke, etc.).

### Required Documents
- Critical illness claim form
- Diagnosis reports from specialists
- Medical test results (pathology, imaging, etc.)
- Hospital admission records
- Treatment plan
- Surgeon's reports (if applicable)

### Diagnosis Requirements
- Must meet policy definition of the illness
- Severity levels often specified
- Early-stage conditions may not qualify
- Specialist confirmation required

### Claims Assessment
- Medical team reviews diagnosis
- Must meet policy criteria
- May require independent medical opinion
- Usually processed within 4-8 weeks

### Payment
- Lump sum payout on approved diagnosis
- Can be used for any purpose
- Tax-free benefit
- No requirement to stop working

## Funeral Cover Claims

### When to Claim
When the insured person passes away and the policy covers funeral expenses.

### Required Documents
- Funeral claim form
- Death certificate
- ID documents
- Proof of banking details
- Funeral cost estimates or invoices

### Fast-Track Process
- Funeral claims are usually prioritized
- Often processed within 48-72 hours
- Cash payments may be available
- Direct payment to funeral parlor possible

### Waiting Periods
- Natural death: Usually 6-month waiting period
- Accidental death: Covered immediately
- Premium refund if death during waiting period

## Car Insurance Claims

### Accident Claims

**Immediate Steps:**
1. Ensure safety of all parties
2. Don't admit fault at the scene
3. Take photos of all vehicles and scene
4. Get details of other parties and witnesses
5. Call police if required
6. Contact your broker immediately

**Required Documents:**
- Completed claim form
- Copy of driver's license
- Police report (if applicable)
- Photos of damage
- Third-party details
- Witness statements
- Repair quotes (3 quotes usually required)

**Claims Process:**
- Assessor inspects damage
- Repairs authorized
- Use approved repair centers
- Car hire arranged if applicable
- Track repair progress

### Theft/Hijacking Claims

**Immediate Steps:**
1. Report to police immediately
2. Get case number
3. Notify your broker within 24 hours
4. Provide vehicle details

**Required Documents:**
- Police case number and report
- Original vehicle registration
- Proof of ownership
- All sets of keys
- Last service records

**Process:**
- Waiting period (usually 30 days) for vehicle recovery
- If recovered: assess damage, authorize repairs
- If not recovered: total loss payout processed
- Settlement based on insured value or market value

## Home Insurance Claims

### Property Damage Claims

**When to Claim:**
- Fire damage
- Storm or flood damage
- Burst geysers
- Impact damage
- Theft-related damage to property

**Immediate Steps:**
1. Secure the property
2. Prevent further damage
3. Take photos/videos
4. Don't dispose of damaged items
5. Get emergency repairs authorized
6. Contact broker immediately

**Required Documents:**
- Claim form
- Photos of damage
- Police report (for theft)
- Repair quotes
- Proof of ownership
- Purchase receipts (if available)

### Contents Claims

**For Theft:**
- Police report and case number
- List of stolen items with values
- Proof of ownership (receipts, photos, serial numbers)
- Bank/credit card statements showing purchases

**For Damage:**
- Photos of damaged items
- Purchase receipts
- Repair quotes or replacement costs
- Assessor's report

### Alternative Accommodation
If your home is uninhabitable:
- Contact broker immediately
- Get authorization before booking
- Keep all receipts
- Reasonable costs usually covered
- Subject to policy limits

## Business Insurance Claims

### Business Interruption
- Submit monthly financial statements
- Proof of lost income
- Business records
- Tax returns
- Accountant's report

### Commercial Property
Similar to home insurance but with:
- Business asset registers
- Stock valuation reports
- Business impact assessments
- Loss of income calculations

### Public Liability
- Incident report
- Photos/evidence
- Witness statements
- Third-party claim details
- Legal correspondence

## Common Reasons Claims Are Rejected

### Non-Disclosure
- Didn't declare pre-existing conditions
- Omitted relevant information on application
- Provided false information

**Prevention:** Always disclose fully and honestly when applying.

### Premium Arrears
- Policy lapsed due to non-payment
- Payment bounced before claim event
- Grace period expired

**Prevention:** Maintain regular premium payments, update banking details.

### Exclusions Apply
- Claim falls under policy exclusions
- Specific circumstances not covered
- Waiting period not completed

**Prevention:** Read and understand your policy document.

### Late Notification
- Didn't report claim within specified timeframe
- Excessive delay in submitting documents

**Prevention:** Report claims immediately, submit documents promptly.

### Fraudulent Claim
- Exaggerated damages
- False information provided
- Staged incidents

**Consequence:** Claim denied, policy voided, possible legal action.

## Appealing a Rejected Claim

### Step 1: Request Written Reasons
- Ask for detailed explanation
- Review against policy terms
- Understand specific grounds for rejection

### Step 2: Gather Additional Evidence
- Obtain missing documentation
- Get expert opinions
- Compile supporting evidence

### Step 3: Formal Appeal
- Submit appeal letter through broker
- Provide all additional evidence
- Request re-assessment
- Set reasonable timeframe for response

### Step 4: Escalation
If appeal unsuccessful:
- Escalate to insurer's complaints department
- Request final response in writing
- Consider independent review

### Step 5: Ombudsman
If still unresolved:
- **Life Insurance:** Ombudsman for Long-term Insurance (011 726 8900)
- **Short-term Insurance:** Ombudsman for Short-term Insurance (011 726 8900)
- Free service for policyholders
- Binding arbitration available

### Step 6: Legal Action
As a last resort:
- Consult with attorney
- Consider costs vs. potential benefit
- Understand time and expense involved

## Claims Tips and Best Practices

### Before You Need to Claim

**1. Know Your Policy:**
- Read policy document thoroughly
- Understand coverage limits
- Note exclusions and waiting periods
- Keep policy details accessible

**2. Keep Records:**
- Save all policy documents
- File premium payment receipts
- Maintain asset registers
- Take photos of valuable items
- Keep purchase receipts

**3. Update Information:**
- Keep contact details current
- Update beneficiaries after life changes
- Notify insurer of address changes
- Review coverage annually

### During the Claims Process

**1. Communicate Clearly:**
- Respond promptly to requests
- Provide complete information
- Ask questions if unsure
- Keep records of all communication

**2. Be Patient but Proactive:**
- Allow reasonable processing time
- Follow up regularly
- Track progress with reference numbers
- Escalate if unreasonable delays

**3. Work With Your Broker:**
- Broker is your advocate
- Provides expert guidance
- Facilitates communication
- Assists with documentation

### After the Claim

**1. Review Settlement:**
- Ensure payment is correct
- Check all items accounted for
- Confirm beneficiaries received funds
- Keep settlement documentation

**2. Maintain Coverage:**
- Continue premium payments
- Update policy if needs changed
- Consider increasing coverage
- Don't cancel impulsively

**3. Learn and Improve:**
- Identify what worked well
- Note areas for improvement
- Update asset records
- Review policy adequacy

## Special Circumstances

### Claiming in Another Country
- Notify insurer immediately
- May need local police reports
- Currency conversion applies
- Additional documentation required
- Processing may take longer

### Claiming After Death of Policyholder
- Executor or beneficiaries can claim
- Letters of executorship may be required
- Estate claims processed differently
- Allow for estate administration time

### Multiple Claims
- Previous claims don't automatically affect new claims
- Each assessed independently
- Pattern of claims may be investigated
- Premiums may increase after multiple claims
