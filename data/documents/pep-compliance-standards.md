## Overview

Compliance at PEP ensures that all products meet legal, safety, ethical, and quality standards. This protects:
- **Customers:** Safe, legal, quality products
- **Brand Reputation:** Trust and credibility
- **Business:** Legal compliance, risk mitigation
- **Suppliers:** Clear expectations and partnerships

This document covers South African regulations, international standards, and PEP-specific policies.

## South African Product Regulations

### 1. Consumer Protection Act (CPA) - Act 68 of 2008

**Key Requirements:**

**Product Safety (Section 61):**
- Products must be safe for intended use
- No hazards to persons or property
- Adequate warnings and instructions

**Product Labeling (Section 24):**
- Country of origin
- Product identity and type
- Name and contact of producer/importer
- Instructions for use (if applicable)
- Warnings (if applicable)

**Right to Return (Section 20):**
- 6-month warranty on all goods (or longer if stated)
- Return within 6 months if defective
- Refund, repair, or replacement

**PEP Implementation:**
- All products screened for safety before import/sale
- Labeling checklist verified by QC team
- Store staff trained on return policy
- Customer complaint mechanism (0800 number)

---

### 2. Compulsory Specifications for Textiles & Clothing

**Governed By:** SABS (South African Bureau of Standards)

**VC 8010: Textile Labeling**

**Mandatory Label Information:**
1. **Fiber Content:** Must state all fibers >5% by weight
   - Example: "65% Cotton, 35% Polyester"
   
2. **Care Instructions:** Washing, drying, ironing symbols
   - Use standard ISO care symbols
   - Language: English or both English + Afrikaans
   
3. **Size:** SA sizing standard or international equivalent
   - Children: Age or height (e.g., "Age 8-9" or "Size 28")
   - Adults: Numeric or letter sizing (e.g., "Size 34" or "Size M")

4. **Country of Origin:** Where manufactured
   - Example: "Made in China" or "Manufactured in South Africa"

5. **Supplier Details:** Name and contact of importer/manufacturer
   - PEP stores (Pty) Ltd, Address, Contact

**Label Placement:**
- Permanently attached (sewn in, not removable sticker)
- Care label at back neckline or side seam
- Fiber content and origin on same or adjacent label

**Non-Compliance Penalties:**
- Fines up to R1,000,000
- Product recall
- Stop-sale order

**PEP QC Process:**
- Pre-production sample: Label approval before bulk production
- Incoming inspection: Verify labels on 100% of shipments
- Store audit: Random checks in stores quarterly

---

### 3. Flammability Standards (SABS 1493)

**Applicable To:** Children's nightwear (ages 0-13)

**Requirements:**
- Must pass flame resistance test
- Low flammability fabric mandatory
- Label must state: "Flame Resistant" or "Low Flammability"

**Testing:**
- Conduct flame test at SABS-approved lab
- Certificate required for each style
- Retain certificates for 5 years

**PEP Application:**
- Only source flame-resistant fabric for kids' sleepwear
- Supplier must provide test certificate
- Label includes flame resistance notice
- Never compromise on this requirement (child safety critical)

---

### 4. Footwear Regulations

**SABS 1133: Footwear Labeling**

**Mandatory Information:**
1. **Material Composition:**
   - Upper: Leather, synthetic, textile
   - Lining: Material type
   - Sole: Rubber, PVC, etc.
   - Use standard material symbols

2. **Size:** European, UK, or US sizing (state which)

3. **Country of Origin:** "Made in..."

4. **Importer Details:** PEP information

5. **Safety Warnings (if applicable):**
   - Example: "Not suitable for wet conditions" (if slip risk)

**Steel Toe Safety Shoes (Industrial):**
- Must meet SANS 20345 standard
- Impact resistance certified
- Not typically sold by PEP (but if ever stocked, certification critical)

---

### 5. Electronics & Electrical Appliances

**SABS LOA (Letter of Authority)**

**Mandatory for:**
- All plug-connected appliances (kettles, irons, fans, chargers, etc.)
- Must have SABS approval mark
- Plug must meet SANS 164 standard (SA 3-pin plug)

**Requirements:**
1. **Electrical Safety Testing:** Insulation, grounding, heat resistance
2. **SABS LOA Certificate:** Supplier must provide
3. **SABS Mark on Product:** Label or imprint on appliance
4. **Compliance Label:** Voltage (220-240V), wattage, frequency (50Hz)

**PEP Process:**
- Only import from suppliers with valid SABS LOA
- Verify LOA certificate before placing PO
- QC checks for SABS mark on every unit
- Non-compliant goods rejected at border

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- Customs seizure (goods won't clear)
- Fines up to R2,000,000
- Criminal prosecution possible

---

### 6. Cellphones & Electronics Safety

**ICASA (Independent Communications Authority of SA)**

**Type Approval Required:**
- All cellphones, tablets, wireless devices
- Ensures electromagnetic compatibility (EMC)
- Prevents harmful interference

**Requirement:**
- Supplier must have ICASA type approval certificate
- ICASA approval number on device or packaging
- Without approval, goods will not clear customs

**PEP Verification:**
- Request ICASA certificate from supplier
- Verify approval number before import
- Maintain certificate files

## Restricted & Prohibited Substances

### 1. REACH Compliance (European Regulation, but adopted by SA for imports)

**Restricted Substances in Textiles & Accessories:**

| Substance | Max Allowed | Found In | Health Risk |
|-----------|------------|----------|-------------|
| **Formaldehyde** | 75 ppm (baby), 300 ppm (adult) | Fabrics (anti-wrinkle) | Skin irritation, allergies |
| **Azo Dyes** | Certain banned (release carcinogens) | Colored textiles | Cancer risk |
| **Heavy Metals** | Varies by metal | Zippers, buttons, jewelry | Poisoning, allergies |
| **Phthalates** | <0.1% | Plastics, footwear | Reproductive harm |
| **Lead** | <90 ppm (children's items) | Paints, coatings | Neurotoxicity |

**Testing:**
- Send samples to accredited lab (SGS, Intertek, Bureau Veritas)
- Test frequency: Every new style before mass production
- Retest if changing supplier or materials

**PEP Policy:**
- Zero tolerance for prohibited substances
- Reject any shipment failing tests
- Supplier bears cost of re-testing and replacement

---

### 2. Children's Product Safety

**Extra Vigilance Required:**

**Choking Hazards:**
- Small parts test for age 0-3: All components must pass "small parts cylinder test"
- Buttons, snaps, embellishments must be securely attached
- Pull test: Minimum 50N force before detachment

**Sharp Edges:**
- No sharp points or edges on zippers, buttons, decorations
- Drawstrings prohibited in children's outerwear (strangulation risk)

**Toxic Materials:**
- Stricter limits on heavy metals for children's products
- No lead in paints or coatings
- BPA-free for any plastic items

**Testing Standards:**
- EN 71 (European Toy Safety) - used as benchmark even for clothing
- ASTM F963 (American) - additional reference
- SANS 197 (South African) - children's nightwear

## Ethical Sourcing & Social Compliance

### PEP Ethical Code of Conduct

**Mandatory for All Suppliers:**

**1. No Child Labor**
- Minimum age: 18 years (or 16 if country law permits with education)
- No workers under 16 in any circumstance
- Age verification required (ID, passport)

**2. No Forced Labor**
- Workers free to leave employment
- No withholding of passports or documents
- No "bonded labor" or debt servitude
- Voluntary overtime only

**3. Fair Wages**
- Minimum: Country legal minimum wage or higher
- Preferably: Living wage (covers basic needs)
- Wages paid on time, in full, with payslips
- No illegal deductions

**4. Working Hours**
- Maximum: 60 hours per week (including overtime)
- Regular: 48 hours or country standard
- Overtime: Voluntary and paid at premium rate
- Rest days: Minimum 1 day per 7-day period

**5. Health & Safety**
- Safe, clean facilities
- Fire exits and equipment
- First aid and medical facilities
- PPE provided where needed (e.g., masks in dusty areas)
- Drinking water and toilets accessible

**6. Freedom of Association**
- Workers can join trade unions (if legal in country)
- Right to collective bargaining
- No retaliation for exercising rights

**7. No Discrimination**
- Equal opportunity regardless of gender, race, religion, age, disability
- Equal pay for equal work
- No harassment or abuse

**8. Environmental Responsibility**
- Wastewater treatment before discharge
- Safe disposal of chemicals and waste
- Energy and water conservation efforts
- Minimize environmental footprint

### Supplier Audits

**Frequency:**
- **New Suppliers:** Audit before first order
- **Existing Suppliers:** Annual audit
- **High-Risk Countries:** Bi-annual audit
- **After Non-Compliance:** Re-audit after 3-6 months

**Audit Process:**
1. **Announced Audit:** Scheduled visit (most common)
2. **Unannounced Audit:** Surprise visit (for high-risk or post-issue)
3. **Semi-Announced:** Week notice but not exact date

**Audit Conducted By:**
- PEP internal team (buyers + QC)
- Third-party auditors (BSCI, WRAP, SMETA)
- Customer audit if PEP is supplying other retailers

**Audit Components:**
- Factory tour (production, warehouses, dorms if applicable)
- Worker interviews (confidential, off-site if possible)
- Document review (payroll, time records, contracts, certifications)
- Management interview

**Audit Rating:**
- **A (Excellent):** Full compliance, best practices
- **B (Good):** Compliant, minor issues
- **C (Acceptable):** Some issues, corrective action plan required
- **D (Poor):** Serious issues, immediate action or suspension
- **E (Fail):** Critical violations, termination

**Critical Violations (Immediate Termination):**
- Child labor found
- Forced labor found
- Physical abuse
- Life-threatening safety hazards
- Bribery or document falsification

### Ethical Certifications

**Recognized Certifications:**

1. **BSCI (Business Social Compliance Initiative)**
   - European standard, widely accepted
   - Covers labor, health & safety, environment

2. **WRAP (Worldwide Responsible Accredited Production)**
   - Focused on apparel and footwear
   - 12 principles covering labor and compliance

3. **SA8000 (Social Accountability International)**
   - Comprehensive social compliance
   - Based on ILO and UN conventions

4. **SMETA (Sedex Members Ethical Trade Audit)**
   - 4-pillar audit: Labor, health & safety, environment, business ethics
   - Shared audits reduce duplication

**PEP Preference:**
- Suppliers with valid certification (within last 12 months) preferred
- Reduces PEP audit burden
- Certificate must be verified (check with issuing body)

## Quality Standards

### 1. Garment Quality Specifications

**Fabric:**
- Weight: As per agreed GSM (grams per square meter)
- Shrinkage: Max 3% after wash (unless pre-shrunk)
- Color fastness: Minimum Grade 3-4 (ISO 105 scale)
- Pilling resistance: Grade 3 minimum

**Construction:**
- Stitching: 10-12 stitches per inch (depending on garment type)
- Seam strength: No breakage under normal stress
- Buttonholes: Clean, secure, correct size
- Zippers: Smooth operation, locked at bottom

**Appearance:**
- No oil stains, marks, or discoloration
- Pattern matching (stripes, prints)
- Symmetry (collars, pockets aligned)
- Loose threads trimmed

**Defect Classification:**

| Defect Type | Examples | Acceptable Qty |
|------------|----------|----------------|
| **Critical** | Safety hazard, totally unwearable | 0% |
| **Major** | Hole, broken seam, wrong size label | <2% |
| **Minor** | Loose thread, slight mark, minor stitch skip | <5% |

---

### 2. Footwear Quality Specifications

**Materials:**
- Upper: As per specification (genuine leather, PU, fabric)
- Sole: Non-slip, durable compound
- Insole: Comfortable, moisture-absorbent
- Stitching: Reinforced at stress points

**Construction:**
- Adhesion test: Sole bonded securely (peel test)
- Flex test: No cracking after 50,000 flexes
- Water resistance: As per claim (if waterproof stated)

**Sizing:**
- Length tolerance: Â±3mm
- Width: As per size chart
- Left and right symmetrical

**Defects:**
- No glue stains visible
- Color consistent (left and right match)
- No scratches or scuffs

---

### 3. Homeware & Electronics Quality

**Textiles (Bedding, Towels):**
- Thread count: As specified
- Shrinkage: Max 5% for towels, 3% for bedding
- Color fastness: Grade 4 minimum
- Seams: Double-stitched and hemmed

**Electronics:**
- Function test: 100% tested before shipment
- Voltage: 220-240V, 50Hz (SA standard)
- Plug: SANS 164 compliant
- Warranty: Minimum 3 months, preferably 12 months
- Packaging: Secure, with manual (English)

## Import & Export Compliance

### 1. Customs Documentation

**Required Documents:**
1. **Commercial Invoice:** Detailed description, values, HS codes
2. **Packing List:** Carton details, weights, dimensions
3. **Bill of Lading (Sea) or Airway Bill (Air):** Shipping document
4. **Certificate of Origin:** Preferential tariff (if applicable)
5. **Import Permit (if required):** Certain controlled goods
6. **SABS LOA:** Electronics and appliances
7. **Test Certificates:** Flammability (kids' sleepwear), restricted substances

**HS Code Classification:**
- Harmonized System for tariff classification
- Correct code critical (determines duty rate)
- Buyer and customs broker verify

**Duty Rates (Examples):**
- Clothing: 40% ad valorem (on FOB + freight)
- Footwear: 30%
- Textiles (fabric): 22%
- Electronics: 0-15% (varies by type)

**VAT:**
- 15% on (FOB + Freight + Duty)
- Reclaimed as input VAT (PEP is VAT vendor)

---

### 2. Customs Clearing Process

**Steps:**
1. Goods arrive at port/airport
2. Customs broker submits docs electronically (SARS system)
3. SARS reviews and assesses duty
4. Payment of duty and VAT
5. Inspection (random or flagged items)
6. Release order issued
7. Goods collected and transported to warehouse

**Clearance Time:**
- Standard: 3-5 days
- Fast-track (if urgent, pre-cleared): 1-2 days
- Delays: If docs incomplete, valuation dispute, inspection required

**Common Issues:**
1. **Incorrect HS Code:** Leads to wrong duty rate, delays
2. **Under-Valuation Suspicion:** SARS questions low prices, requires proof
3. **Missing Certificates:** SABS LOA not submitted, goods held
4. **Prohibited Goods:** Counterfeits, banned substances detected

**PEP Best Practices:**
- Use experienced customs broker
- Submit accurate, complete documents
- Maintain open communication with broker
- Resolve issues proactively (don't ignore)

## Intellectual Property Compliance

### 1. Trademarks & Brands

**PEP Policy: No Counterfeits**
- Never source counterfeit or fake branded goods
- Even if supplier offers, always refuse
- Legal and reputational risk too high

**Verification:**
- If sourcing branded items (legitimate), verify brand authorization
- Supplier must provide: Brand authorization letter, licensing agreement
- Confirm with brand owner if any doubt

**Consequences of Counterfeiting:**
- Customs seizure (goods destroyed)
- Criminal prosecution
- Fines and imprisonment
- Brand lawsuits (massive damages)
- Reputation destruction

---

### 2. Design & Copyright

**Own Designs:**
- PEP owns copyright on designs created internally or commissioned
- Supplier cannot sell same design to competitors
- Non-disclosure agreement (NDA) with suppliers

**Supplier Designs:**
- Clarify ownership before production
- If supplier owns, negotiate exclusivity (if strategic)
- Respect supplier's IP (don't copy without permission)

**Third-Party Designs:**
- Licensed characters, prints (e.g., Disney, sports teams)
- Requires licensing agreement
- Royalty payments
- PEP rarely does this (cost and complexity)

## FAQs

**Q: Can we import clothing without fiber content labels?**  
A: No. It's illegal. Goods will be stopped at customs or risk fines in-store.

**Q: Supplier says SABS LOA is expensive and not necessary. Is that true?**  
A: False. It's legally mandatory for electrical appliances. Without LOA, goods won't clear customs. Non-negotiable.

**Q: What if we find child labor during an audit?**  
A: Immediate actions: 1) Document with photos/evidence, 2) Notify compliance officer and legal, 3) Terminate supplier relationship, 4) Ensure children are safely transitioned (not just fired), 5) Report to authorities if required.

**Q: Can we sell goods that fail quality inspection at a discount?**  
A: Depends on severity. Minor cosmetic defects: Possible with clear labeling ("seconds"). Major defects or safety issues: No, must be destroyed or returned to supplier.

**Q: How do I know if my product needs ICASA approval?**  
A: If it has a radio transmitter (cellphones, Wi-Fi devices, Bluetooth), it needs ICASA approval. Ask supplier for certificate.

**Q: What is AQL and how do I apply it?**  
A: AQL (Acceptable Quality Level) is a statistical sampling method. PEP typically uses AQL 2.5 (means max 2.5% major defects acceptable). QC team trained on sampling tables.
